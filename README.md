# Airbnb vs Serviced Apartment in Dhaka
_What No One Tells You Before You Book_

If you’re planning a stay in Dhaka. Whether for a few days or a few months. You’ve probably faced this exact dilemma: Airbnb or a serviced apartment?

On paper, both look pretty similar. Fully furnished, flexible stays, decent locations. But once you actually start living there, the differences become very real especially in areas like Bashundhara Residential Area, Gulshan, and Dhanmondi.

Let’s talk about the reality not the polished listing photos.

## Key Takeaways
*   Airbnb works best for short, casual stays, but can be unpredictable.
*   Serviced apartments feel more like a controlled, professional setup.
*   What looks cheaper at first (Airbnb) often isn’t cheaper in the end.
*   In areas like Gulshan and Bashundhara, serviced apartments usually offer better security and consistency.

## What It’s Actually Like Living There

### 1. The Price Trap Is Real
You’ll see a nice Airbnb in Dhanmondi at a great nightly rate and think, “This is perfect.”
Then come the extras:
*   Cleaning fees
*   Platform service charges
*   Sometimes even separate utility bills

By the end, you’re paying way more than expected.
[Serviced apartments](https://www.hotels.com.bd/residential-apartment-service/) especially around Gulshan don’t play that game. The price you see is usually what you pay, and it often includes everything from Wi-Fi to maintenance.

### 2. Airbnb Can Feel Like a Gamble
Let’s be honest Airbnb quality in Dhaka is inconsistent.
In Bashundhara Residential Area, for example, you might find:
*   A clean, modern flat with a helpful host
*   Or a place that looks great online but feels neglected in person

It’s hit or miss.
Serviced apartments are built for consistency. Same setup, same standards, same support. You don’t have to “hope” it’s good. It just is (most of the time).

| Feature | Airbnb | Serviced Apartment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cost** | Looks cheaper initially | Higher but fixed |
| **Bills** | Often separate | Usually included |
| **Cleaning** | Not always regular | Routine housekeeping |
| **Support** | Depends on host | Dedicated staff |
| **Security** | Varies a lot | More structured |
| **Experience** | Personal, sometimes uneven | Standard and predictable |
| **Best For** | Short visits | Long stays & work trips |

### 3. Security Isn’t Equal Everywhere
This is something many people overlook.
In Gulshan, buildings tend to have strict entry systems, guards, and better overall control especially in serviced apartments.

With Airbnb, security depends entirely on the building:
*   Some are well-guarded
*   Others are just regular apartments with minimal oversight

If you’re staying long-term or with family, that difference becomes important very quickly.

### 4. Daily Comfort Feels Different
Airbnb can feel cozy but also a bit improvised.
You might run into:
*   Missing kitchen basics
*   Locked storage areas
*   Furniture that looks nice but isn’t practical

Serviced apartments are more intentional. Everything is set up for everyday living especially in older areas like Dhanmondi, where many units have been upgraded specifically for comfort.

### 5. When Something Breaks…
And it will. This is Dhaka.
*   AC stops cooling
*   Internet slows down
*   Power backup doesn’t kick in

**With Airbnb:**
You text the host… and wait.

**With a serviced apartment:**
You call reception or maintenance, and someone usually shows up.
That difference feels small until you’re dealing with it in real life.

### 6. So, Which One Should You Pick?
It really depends on your situation.

**Choose Airbnb if:**
*   You’re staying short-term
*   You want flexibility
*   You don’t mind a bit of uncertainty

**Choose a serviced apartment if:**
*   You’re staying longer than a few weeks
*   You’re working or need stability
*   You prefer a smoother, hassle-free experience

In places like Gulshan, most professionals don’t even debate this anymore. They go straight for serviced apartments because it just makes life easier.

## Conclusion
Airbnb in Dhaka can look attractive more choices, lower prices, more “home-like.” But it often comes with trade-offs: inconsistency, hidden costs, and less support.

[Serviced apartments](https://www.banglamart.com/co-living) cost more upfront, but they remove a lot of friction from daily life. And in a city where small inconveniences can quickly turn into big ones, that reliability matters.

At the end of the day, it’s not just about saving money. It’s about how smoothly you want your stay to go.
